As my gaze traversed the bathroom landscape a shame filled realization dawned:
  1. I sat and shat before the presence of shitpaper was verified.
Denial began its work, forcing an intensive scan of the area.
  • There, on the floor beside the toilet, a mostly gone roll whose last remains lay unwound in a messy heap.
  • Three year olds are excellent mischief makers, like drunken leprochauns who tirelessly roam the house laying waste to ordered entities; this roll of paper poop squares had no chance against such a foe.
As I looked down on the crumpled tissues and contemplated my situation with disgust a bolt of lightning struck my mind: just how clean does that have to be before it is put to work scrubbing my anus?
Perhaps I have been prudish with my pucky paper. No longer. What a freeing realization.
